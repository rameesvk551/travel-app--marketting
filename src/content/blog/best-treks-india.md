# 10 Best Treks in India for 2026

India is a trekker's paradise. From the snow-capped Himalayas to the lush Western Ghats, there's a trail for every fitness level and every season. Here are our top picks for 2026.

## Beginner-Friendly Treks

### 1. Kedarkantha (Uttarakhand)
- **Altitude**: 3,810m
- **Duration**: 4 days
- **Difficulty**: Easy-Moderate
- **Best Time**: Dec — April
- **Cost**: ₹6,500 approx.

The perfect first Himalayan trek. Summit day rewards you with a 360° view of snow peaks, and the campsites are among the most scenic in India.

### 2. Triund (Himachal Pradesh)
- **Altitude**: 2,828m
- **Duration**: 1-2 days
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Best Time**: March — November
- **Cost**: ₹2,500 approx.

A classic weekend trek from McLeodganj. Short enough for beginners, stunning enough for experienced trekkers. The sunset view of the Dhauladhar range is unforgettable.

### 3. Brahmatal (Uttarakhand)
- **Altitude**: 3,475m
- **Duration**: 5 days
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: Dec — March
- **Cost**: ₹7,500 approx.

A winter wonderland trek with frozen lakes and panoramic views of Mt. Trishul and Nanda Ghunti.

## Intermediate Treks

### 4. Hampta Pass (Himachal Pradesh)
- **Altitude**: 4,270m
- **Duration**: 5 days
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: June — October
- **Cost**: ₹8,500 approx.

The dramatic crossover from lush Kullu valley to barren Lahaul makes this one of the most varied treks in India.

### 5. Valley of Flowers (Uttarakhand)
- **Altitude**: 3,658m
- **Duration**: 6 days
- **Difficulty**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: July — September
- **Cost**: ₹9,200 approx.

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with over 600 species of wildflowers. Combine with Hemkund Sahib for a spiritual experience.

### 6. Roopkund (Uttarakhand)
- **Altitude**: 4,800m
- **Duration**: 7 days
- **Difficulty**: Moderate-Hard
- **Cost**: ₹11,000 approx.

The mysterious "Skeleton Lake" trek. Challenging but incredibly rewarding, with alpine meadows and glacial landscapes.

## Expert-Level Treks

### 7. Chadar Trek (Ladakh)
- **Altitude**: 3,390m
- **Duration**: 9 days
- **Difficulty**: Difficult
- **Best Time**: Jan — Feb
- **Cost**: ₹18,500 approx.

Walk on a frozen river in sub-zero temperatures. This is the ultimate bucket-list trek.

### 8. Goechala (Sikkim)
- **Altitude**: 4,940m
- **Duration**: 10 days
- **Difficulty**: Difficult
- **Cost**: ₹15,000 approx.

Face-to-face with Kanchenjunga, the third-highest mountain in the world. Rhododendron forests and high-altitude passes.

### 9. Stok Kangri (Ladakh)
- **Altitude**: 6,153m
- **Duration**: 7 days
- **Difficulty**: Very Difficult
- **Cost**: ₹22,000 approx.

One of the highest trekkable peaks in India. Requires prior high-altitude experience and good fitness.

### 10. Pin Parvati Pass (Himachal)
- **Altitude**: 5,319m
- **Duration**: 11 days
- **Difficulty**: Very Difficult
- **Cost**: ₹16,000 approx.

The ultimate Himalayan crossing. From the green Pin Valley to the glacier-carved Parvati Valley.

## Essential Trekking Tips

1. **Start training 2 months before** — focus on cardio and leg strength
2. **Acclimatize properly** — never rush altitude gain
3. **Invest in good shoes** — your feet will thank you
4. **Pack light** — every gram counts at altitude
5. **Go with reputable operators** — safety first
